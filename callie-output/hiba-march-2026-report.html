Overview
The Big Picture

Your emails are performing well above industry average — and a key metric we flagged in February just proved the work is paying off.

March was a quieter month for list growth — 24 new profiles, down from February. The most likely reason: fewer events. November and January were packed with open houses, discovery walks, and community events — months when high-intent families show up and sign up. March had less of that, and organic signups reflect it. The website popup was also non-functional for part of the month, which added some headwind, but that's secondary to the event calendar.

The genuine win in March: the welcome series click rate. In February, we flagged the click rate as too low at 2.78% and made changes to the flow. March results are proving it out — click rate jumped to 11.32%. That's a 4× improvement. The work is paying off.

Behind the scenes: the Ravenna → Klaviyo integration went live. Applicant data now syncs automatically — no manual uploads, no gaps. This is the technical foundation for everything that comes next. Speaking of which, the Long-Term Nurture Flow is the next major build — the three-track system that takes families from welcome series exit all the way to application.

Long-Term Nurture Flow

The Long-Term Nurture Flow is the next major build. After families complete the welcome series, they'll enter one of three tracks based on start year and event attendance — Track A1 pushes non-event families toward attending, Track A2 moves event attendees toward completing their Ravenna application, and Track B nurtures 2027+ families with a longer-term relationship approach. With the Ravenna integration now live and syncing applicant data automatically, the segmentation to power these tracks is ready.
