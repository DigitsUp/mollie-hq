What's workingVolume & engagement

  • Reach +45.9% (14.7M total recipients), email Unique Clicks +71.2%, SMS Unique Clicks +133.5% — engagement is scaling.
  • SMS Unique Conversions +34.1% — SMS is converting and growing.
  • Email AOV +7.9% — quality demand intact.

Needs attentionEconomics & context

  • Campaign revenue −23.6% — tracks directly with Shopify orders −25%. Campaigns did not underperform the market — they tracked it. The reach story (+45.9%) and engagement story (Unique Clicks +71.2% email, +133.5% SMS) are real wins.
  • Shopify returns +37% business-wide — an operational issue affecting all channels, not a campaign quality issue.

OpportunitiesThe path forward

  • Tighter segmentation over bigger blasts (split by major 3: new / loyal / general).
  • Rebuild 2025's multi-touch reminder cadence — each reminder drove $19–26K.
  • Personalized Send Time (PST) — 7 of 26 Q1 campaigns ran with PST (27% of sends). Revenue quality was higher; the $21K Spring Savings send stands out. PST is a revenue quality play — apply selectively to high-value, sale-driven sends in Q2.
  • Lean into AOV strength with bundle & higher-ticket sends.

§ 08

Q1 in summary
& Q2 priorities

The quarter ended with momentum — March flipped positive, cohort quality improved, and SMS emerged as a real second channel. Q2 is about diagnosing, rebuilding, and leaning in.

What's workingThe wins

  • Klaviyo held share — down only −10.4% while Shopify orders fell −25%. SMS +63%, now 34% of attributed revenue. March cohort retention 10.73% — best in 8 months. AOV held and grew. The program protected the business when it needed it most.
  • SMS +63%, now 34% of attributed revenue.
  • March Shopify cohort retention 10.73% — best in 8 months.
  • Shopify AOV +5%, campaign email AOV +7.9%.

Needs attentionThe context

  • Core issue is demand — Shopify orders −25%, new customers −27%, returning −24%. → Q2: tighter segmentation, frequency management.
  • Campaign revenue softer — tracked the market (−23.6% vs orders −25%). → Q2: rebuild reminder cadence, apply PST to high-value sends.
  • Email flow RPR soft — fewer new customers compresses Welcome Series. → Q2: diagnose Welcome by source, refresh core flows.
  • SMS list contraction — March near-breakeven; SMS churn is already reversing. → Q2: tighter segmentation, SMS churn continues to improve.

OpportunitiesQ2 direction

  • Diagnose Welcome Series by acquisition source before changes.
  • Rebuild campaign reminder cadence — apply PST to high-value, sale-driven sends.
  • Scale inventory-triggered flows.
  • Push SMS toward 40%+ share by end of Q2.
