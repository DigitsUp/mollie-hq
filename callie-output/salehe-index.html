03
Warm

Earn the inbox before we use it.

Cold lists trigger spam filters the moment a new sender starts hammering them. To avoid that, we segment by ISP (Gmail, Yahoo, Apple, Outlook) and start small. Sends are engineered to pull clicks and engagement signals, not to push hard offers.

This builds your sender reputation deliberately. By the time a campaign goes out, your domain has a track record with the mailbox providers, and the campaign actually lands.

Honest

A note on what could go wrong — and how we fix it.

What can go wrong

Cold lists are unpredictable. We can clean them carefully, warm them properly, and they still sometimes don’t engage at the level needed to protect a sender reputation long term. If that’s where we land thirty days in, you’ll hear it directly from us. Not in a quarterly review. Not buried in a report.

There’s a version of this where we stop the cold sends, finish the foundation, and you grow the list organically from there. A giveaway. A post. A campaign driving sign-ups to a clean welcome flow. You have the reach to do that, and starting from clean organic engagement is a faster path to sustainable deliverability than fighting through a list that doesn’t want to engage.

How we fix it

Split the list by domain provider — Gmail, Yahoo, Outlook — to isolate where deliverability issues are coming from and contain them. Leverage your social reach for organic fresh signups: you have the audience to build clean engagement fast. Additionally, using a third-party ESP to send the cold list from can help preserve your new Klaviyo account while we assess performance. We’ll pivot once we have real data and insights. The plan adapts. That’s the point.
