The Gap

BrandFeatured sells a one-time product to customers who should be buying quarterly.

Every touchpoint on the site is acquisition-only. The current email program is transactional at best — stage updates that read like receipts, no relationship, no retention, no reason to come back.

Meanwhile, the brands that compound their PR coverage — publishing every quarter, timed to seasonal moments — see outsized SEO gains, higher social proof scores, and measurably stronger conversion rates. BrandFeatured should be the one telling that story. Right now, nobody is.

Gap 01
Pipeline emails are receipts, not relationships
Stage updates (Briefing, Writing, Draft, Published) have zero brand voice. The "You're live" moment — the highest-leverage touchpoint BrandFeatured has — is currently a status notification.
Gap 02
No lifecycle after the campaign ends
Nothing fires after Published. No post-purchase nurture. No seasonal re-engagement. No win-back. Buyers who should be on their third campaign have gone silent.
Gap 03
High-intent drop-off with no recovery
PR is a considered purchase. Visitors land on the pricing page, hesitate, and leave. No site abandonment flow. No checkout recovery. These are the warmest leads on the site — and they're walking out quietly.
Gap 04
One message to every vertical
A DTC founder and a restaurant operator have entirely different PR calendars. Right now they get the same email. Vertical segmentation unlocks seasonal relevance that generic sends can never achieve.

Platform Setup

Two platforms. One clear owner each.

MailerLite stays active and handles everything tied to the PR campaign pipeline. Klaviyo is introduced alongside it to own acquisition, retention, and re-engagement. The two platforms don't overlap — each has a defined lane.

Developer Setup Requirements
Seven prerequisites before any flow goes live. Items marked DEV REQUIRED need active development work — others are configuration only.
01
Klaviyo Account + Domain Auth
Create Klaviyo account. Add SPF, DKIM, and DMARC records to BrandFeatured DNS. Required before any email can send. Allow 24–48hrs for DNS propagation.
02
Subscriber Data Migration
Export active subscribers, custom fields, segments, and suppression data (unsubscribes, bounces, spam complaints) from MailerLite. Import into Klaviyo with correct status flags to maintain compliance.
03
ThriveCart → Klaviyo Integration
Connect via ThriveCart's native Klaviyo integration (Settings → Integrations → Klaviyo). Passes: Placed Order, Ordered Product, Cancelled Order, Refunded Order. Do not connect Stripe directly — causes duplicate events.
04
Klaviyo JS — Behavioral Tracking DEV LIKELY NOT REQUIRED
ThriveCart's native integration does not pass behavioral events. Custom Klaviyo JS may be needed on brandfeatured.com for: Active on Site, Started Checkout, Viewed Item. DigitsUp will confirm within Week 1 whether custom implementation is needed or if ThriveCart can cover this differently.
05
"Publishing Complete" Custom Event DEV REQUIRED
When a campaign finishes publishing, send a custom event to Klaviyo to trigger the Post-Purchase flow at the correct moment. Fires from BrandFeatured's internal system when Status = Published. Implementation outline provided by DigitsUp within Week 1.
06
Replace MailerLite Popup
Disable existing MailerLite popup on brandfeatured.com. Replace with new 3-step Klaviyo popup. MailerLite embedded forms feeding pipeline lists (order forms etc.) stay as-is — do not remove.
07
SMS Compliance (A2P 10DLC)
Enable Klaviyo SMS, register sending number, add compliant opt-in language to popup Step 3. US sends require A2P 10DLC carrier registration — allow 1–2 weeks for approval before SMS flows can activate.
