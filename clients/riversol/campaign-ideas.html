01 · NEW

Bundle Math Email

Some of your best customers bought three products individually when a bundle would have saved them $30+. They don't know they overpaid. Show them. "You bought the Cleanser, Serum, and Moisturizing Concentrate separately — the Anti-Aging Trio Bundle would have saved you $31." No guilt, just math. Then offer them the bundle as their next purchase at a slight loyalty discount.

Segment: Bought 3+ matching bundle products individually in last 90d Timing: 14 days post-3rd qualifying purchase
Why it works: Customers who bought individually are already proven buyers at full price. The "overpaid" framing is a retention signal, not a discount mechanic — it shows you're looking out for them.

SMS-Only Flash — Exclusive to Text Subscribers

Every SMS campaign they run mirrors an email campaign — confirmed across 300+ sends. There are zero SMS-exclusive offers in the data. Run one: "This deal is SMS-only — never in email." 24-hour window, one product, light offer (free shipping or complimentary travel-size). The exclusivity is the hook. Announce it by saying "This will never show up in your inbox." This also trains the list to value the channel.

Segment: Full SMS subscriber list Timing: Mid-month gap, no active sale
Why now: SMS-exclusive offers are the single most reliable driver of SMS engagement and list growth. You're giving people a reason to stay subscribed that has nothing to do with email.

09 · UPGRADE

Winback Automation — Build the Full Sequence

A winback flow exists and triggers at 95/105 days post-purchase. But it's a single moment, and its click rate in Q1 2026 is only 2.4% on 73K delivered — underperforming. The manual "Customer Winback - Standard" isn't live at all. The real opportunity: build a proper 3-touch automated sequence (90d / 120d / 150d), each with escalating offers, before defaulting to the deep-discount 40% OFF blast they're currently using as a first resort.

Segment: Lapsed FP (90/120/150d), Lapsed SNC Timing: Automated — replace current single-touch trigger
Why now: Getting to a customer at 90 days costs far less (and requires far less discount) than hitting them at 150 days with 40% off. The single-touch flow is better than nothing but leaves revenue on the table at every earlier touchpoint.

⚠️ The Big Picture: More Volume, Less Revenue Per Send

  • Revenue is down 16% YoY despite sending 43% more email — the list is bigger but each send is generating less. More campaigns, more saturation, lower AOV.

  • Order count is up 37% — so the list is transacting more frequently, just at lower values. Volume play vs value play.
  • Flow revenue also down: $165K in Q1 2026 vs $378K in Q1 2025 — the sample flow restructure removed a major revenue-driving sequence.

✅ Flow Observations — Q1 2026 vs Q1 2025

  • Flow revenue collapsed from $378K → $165K (−56%) — almost entirely explained by two missing flows: Welcome Series ($125K in 2025, appears restructured/replaced) and Sample Post-Delivery flow ($110K, was 707K volume in 2025).
  • Welcome Series was the highest-AOV flow in 2025 at $112 per order and 17.5% click — whatever replaced it in 2026 is not appearing in the top flows. Worth identifying and benchmarking against 2025 performance.
  • Abandoned Cart was the 3rd highest revenue flow in Q1 2025 ($71K, $115 AOV, 20.3% click) — not showing in Q1 2026 top flows. This is a significant gap. Need to confirm it's live and triggering.
  • Riversol+ ecosystem is the bright spot: Reminders + Recharges + Welcome = $98K combined in Q1 2026. This didn't exist in Q1 2025. Subscription is growing and generating meaningful revenue.
  • Replenishment AOV is strong — $84 in V2, $133 in the original (2025). These buyers purchase higher-value orders when they come back. The replenishment flow should be the highest-priority automation to optimize.
  • Winback flow AOV = $60 — decent, but 73K delivered for only 100 orders (0.14% conversion). Even a 2x improvement here would be meaningful.
