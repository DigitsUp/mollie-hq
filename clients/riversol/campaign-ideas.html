10 · EXPAND

Tighten the Winback Cadence

Their current winback is a 4-email burst in late Dec–early Jan (8 months no purchase). That's too late and too seasonal. Build a rolling winback: Email 1 at 90 days (gentle, no discount), Email 2 at 120 days ("your skin is probably telling you something"), Email 3 at 150 days (best-value offer). Then suppress. The Dec 12 "40% OFF WB" for unsuppressed SNC suggests they know deep discounts exist for this — build the full funnel so you hit them before it gets to 40% off.

Segment: Lapsed FP (90/120/150d), Lapsed SNC Timing: Ongoing automated trigger
Why now: Earlier intervention = cheaper recovery. Getting to a customer at 90 days costs far less (and requires far less discount) than waiting until 8 months.

Almost-Tier Buyer

Some of your most valuable customers are sitting $25–$30 away from Gold or Silver — and they don't know it. Don't show them a generic progress bar. Show them the exact dollar amount between them and their next tier, and make the specific benefit real. "You're $27 from Gold. 12 · NEW

Password Flash Sale — SMS Opt-In Mechanic

Send an email to customers who are email-only (no SMS). Tell them there's a 48-hour sale — but the access code is locked behind a text. "Text RIVERSOL to [#] to unlock your offer." They opt into SMS, receive the code immediately, and now they're on both channels. The email plays it cool: "We're trying something different. This one's for the text subscribers." No pressure. Pure curiosity mechanic.

Segment: Email-only subscribers (no SMS consent) Timing: Mid-month quiet period
Why now: SMS subscribers have significantly higher purchase rates. This converts email-only customers to dual-channel at low cost — no paid ads, no list buy, just a clever mechanic using your existing audience.
