🔍 How to Read These Numbers

  • Revenue is down 26% YoY despite sending 24% more email. More volume, less return per send — the story is in AOV compression and two missing flow sequences.

Campaign Performance — Q1 2026 vs Q1 2025

Campaign Review

Q1 2026 campaigns generated $949K across 15,157 attributed orders — up 37% in order volume but down 16% in revenue vs Q1 2025. The primary driver of the gap is offer depth: Q1 2025 campaigns averaged $102 AOV compared to $63 in Q1 2026. Lighter promotional structures (tiered thresholds, free-gift mechanics) drove more transactions at lower basket sizes.

March was the strongest month ($461K) anchored by Spend & Save and the Spring Sale running concurrently — but two overlapping major events may have split audience attention and diluted individual campaign performance. The Q1 2025 equivalent (Spring Sale + Customer Appreciation Sale) drove $538K on less volume.

Bright spots: survey and feedback campaigns consistently delivered above-average click rates (3–7%), and the Calm Edit newsletter generated $34K across three sends with zero promotional offers.

Automation Performance — Q1 2026 vs Q1 2025

Flow / Automation Review

Q1 2026 flow revenue came in at $165K across 5,664 orders — down 56% from $378K in Q1 2025. The decline is largely structural rather than performance-based. Two high-volume sequences that drove significant Q1 2025 revenue were either restructured or are absent: the post-sample delivery flow ($110K, 707K delivered) and the abandoned cart flow ($71K, $115 AOV, 20.3% click rate).

The Riversol+ ecosystem is the clear bright spot: Reminders, Recharges, and Welcome flows combined generated $98K in Q1 2026 — revenue that didn't exist in Q1 2025. Subscription is a growing and healthy revenue layer. However, the program is showing churn pressure in Q1 2026 that's worth monitoring closely.

The winback flow (95/105 days post-purchase) is the highest-volume non-transactional flow at 73K delivered, but its 2.4% click rate and $6K revenue output signals it needs a rebuild — either deeper sequencing or a stronger offer structure.

02 · P1 · CONFIRMED GAP

Winback Flow — Upgrade from Single Touch to 3-Step Sequence

The current winback flow fires once at 95/105 days post-purchase. Q1 2026 performance: 73K delivered, 2.4% click rate, 100 orders, $5,964 revenue. That's a 0.14% order conversion rate on a warm audience. The standard "Customer Winback - Standard" flow with Email + SMS is sitting on manual — it's built but not live. A proper 3-step sequence (90d soft check-in / 120d offer / 150d last-chance) with escalating urgency would recover customers before they hit the deep-discount rescue stage.

Current: Single touch at 95/105d · 73K delivered · $60 AOV · 2.4% click
Opportunity: 3x as many touchpoints across 60-day window before needing 40% off
Manual flow exists: "Customer Winback - Standard (Email & SMS)" — ready to activate

03 · P1 · RIV+ RETENTION

Failed Charge Recovery Sequence

19% of Riversol+ charge attempts are failing — that's 3,924 failed charges in Q1 2026 alone and rising (22.5% in March). The "Riversol+ Failed Charge (Subscribfy)" flow exists and is live, but with only 3,527 delivered and a 20.2% click rate, it doesn't appear to be fully recovering the audience. At $19/month per member, 3,924 failed charges = ~$74K in missed recurring revenue per quarter. A proper dunning sequence (immediate / +3 days / +7 days with card-update CTA) could recover a meaningful portion of this.

Reporting

Klaviyo's conversion attribution includes Riversol+ $19/month membership charges as "orders." If you're comparing to a Shopify revenue number, these two figures won't reconcile cleanly. Understanding your source of truth helps us report on the right metric.

02
Riversol+

What drove the November 2025 spike (5,429 new Riversol+ members)?

November saw 5x the monthly average for new Riv+ signups. If this was a BFCM promotional push, most of those members appear to have churned — Q1 2026 is showing net negative membership. Understanding what the offer was helps us assess whether Riv+ acquisition needs a different strategy.

03
Riversol+

What's the target Riversol+ membership base, and how is success measured?

Q1 2026 is showing net churn (−1,550 members) with a 19% failed charge rate. Is there a target active member count? A retention rate goal? Understanding the Riv+ KPIs helps frame whether the current trajectory is a concern or within expected range for a growing subscription program.
