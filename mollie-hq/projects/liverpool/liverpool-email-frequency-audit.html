Key Findings & Recommendations

  • 🔴
    Day 1 is the worst collision point — up to 5 emails in a single day

    Welcome flow + Browse abandonment + Order Confirmation + PP #1 + Wonderment Shipment Created all fire simultaneously when a customer subscribes and purchases on Day 1. This is the highest fatigue risk in the entire journey. Fix: Add a 24h smart send delay to Browse abandonment if Welcome was sent in the last 24h. PP #1 should suppress if Welcome was sent <4h ago.

  • 🔴
    Welcome flow Email #4 (Day 5) consistently collides with campaigns

    3 of 10 profiles received 2 emails on Day 5: Welcome Flow Email #4 + a campaign. The welcome flow doesn't appear to have a campaign exclusion window. Fix: Add a 1-day smart send exclusion to welcome flow emails — if a campaign was sent in the last 24h, skip or delay.

  • 🔴
    AM + PM sale emails on same day (April 5)

    The "Last Chance" campaign was sent twice on the same day — AM and PM sends — to 4 of 10 profiles. This appears intentional (urgency) but is the most likely unsubscribe driver. Fix: Evaluate if PM resend is filtered to non-openers only. If not, add that segmentation immediately.

  • 🟡
    Repeat buyers hit compounding flow overlap

    Customers who purchase multiple times in 30 days (like chriscrowls67) trigger multiple simultaneous Wonderment sequences, PP flows, and review flows — hitting 4 emails on a single day. Fix: Add a global 24h sending frequency cap in Klaviyo, or at minimum suppress Wonderment "Carrier Picked Up" if a marketing email was sent in the last 6h.

  • 🟡
    Email SMS Recruiter stacks on PP and Review flows

    SMS Recruiter fires at Day 15 and Day 23 — both of which also have PP flow emails and in some cases Review flows active. This creates 3-email days with different asks (SMS opt-in, come back incentive, leave a review). Fix: Check if SMS Recruiter has exclusion logic for recent PP emails. If not, add a 48h suppression window.

  • 🟢
    Non-converters (abandoned checkout only) receive near-daily campaigns by Week 2

    eidlej received 17 emails in 30 days without purchasing, including 3 abandoned checkout emails and then 14 campaign sends. No additional flow exclusions appear to kick in for non-converters after the AC sequence ends. Fix: Consider a new customer suppression segment that throttles campaigns to every 3 days for the first 30 days.
