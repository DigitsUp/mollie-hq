Recommended Actions & Q2 Priorities
01
Riversol+ idle credits — email + SMS now
3,260 members with CA$19+ unspent. Send email first: "Your credits are waiting." Follow with SMS 48 hours later for non-openers. Fastest revenue available — days, not weeks.
02
Dormant loyalists points reactivation
36,174 customers with 350+ points. Email with points-expiry urgency or double-points event. Add SMS for non-openers at Day 3. No discount needed — points are the incentive.
03
Add SMS to sample post-delivery flow
The 44-day conversion window is the critical nurture period. An SMS at Day 15 and Day 30 anchored around the Anti-Aging Trio and Free Retinol Neck Cream offer could meaningfully lift the 1.36% trial-to-paid conversion rate.
04
One-Hit Wonder winback — email + SMS sequence
32,280 customers. Three emails escalating: free shipping → 15% off → GWP. If no conversion after Email 3, send one SMS as the final close. Find the trigger without leading with the deepest discount.
05
Test bundle-anchored campaigns with SMS support
Anti-Aging Trio down 46–52%. Run a bundle-focused campaign (GWP or spend-and-save) and pair with an SMS send to Riversol+ members and high-intent segments. SMS for bundle moments converts faster than email alone.
06
Skin analyzer: welcome series A/B test
Benchmark offer-led welcome (promo code) vs. skin analyzer path (personalization, no promo). The analyzer is a no-discount entry point that could reduce margin erosion while improving subscriber quality. Monitor Q2 for list growth and first-purchase conversion delta.
07
Scale Riversol+ membership to grow the best-converting flow
Riversol+ Reminders has a 16.8% placed order rate — 3x better than any other flow. Every new member added directly increases the size and revenue of this audience. Membership growth is a Klaviyo strategy, not just a subscription play.
08
Build second-purchase flow for organic first-timers
8,157 customers who trusted Riversol enough to pay without a trial. Personalized to their first product — serum buyers get moisturizer next, Trio buyers get Eye Repair Treatment. Add SMS at Day 30 as the trigger. This segment has the highest brand affinity of any group.
09
Q4 2026 — Plan a structured BFCM strategy now
November 2025 was the strongest cohort on the board — CA$153K in first order revenue from only 12.7K customers, with a CA$10.94 first order AOV vs. CA$2–4 every other month. BFCM drives high-intent buyers who skip the trial and buy bundles at full price. This is the model to replicate. Building a BFCM campaign calendar now — not in October — is the single highest-leverage Q4 investment.

Business Snapshot — Key Takeaways
  • Traffic is up slightly (+3%) but conversion has nearly halved (5.8% → 3.19%), which is the single biggest driver of order and revenue decline. This points to a top-of-funnel issue — likely fewer trial-to-paid conversions rather than a drop in site quality.

3 — Retention & Cohort Key Takeaways
  • Month 1 is consistently the strongest repeat purchase window across every cohort — customers who buy in Month 1 are your highest-value segment and should be the primary target of lifecycle flows in the 30-60 day window post first purchase.
  • Q1 2026 cohort first order revenue is significantly lower than comparable 2025 cohorts at similar volume. Jan 2026 (23.1K customers) generated CA$59.7K at first order vs. Apr 2025 (23.7K customers) at CA$98.2K — a 39% drop at nearly identical customer volume. This confirms AOV compression at the point of first conversion, not just in email.
  • The CA$2-4 first order AOV across all cohorts confirms the vast majority of "first orders" are sample orders — not product purchases. This is expected given the trial model, but it means the cohort data is essentially measuring sample takers, not product buyers, at the first order level.
  • Month 1 AOV for Q1 2026 cohorts is healthy and comparable to 2025 — Jan 2026 at CA$64.21 and Feb 2026 at CA$69.32 are in line with Apr 2025 at CA$64.02. The people who do reach Month 1 are spending at similar levels. The lifecycle is not broken — it's volume-starved.
  • November 2025 is the strongest cohort on the board — CA$153K in first order revenue from only 12.7K customers, with a CA$10.94 first order AOV vs. the CA$2–4 seen in every other month. This is almost certainly Black Friday / Cyber Monday: BFCM drives high-intent buyers who skip the free trial entirely and go straight to bundled product purchases at full price. Infrastructure
How the Streams Connect
Suppressed Profile Recovery

Both streams include a suppressed profile recovery layer. Recoverable suppressed profiles (~27K FP, ~698K SNC) are unsuppressed in controlled batches and fed into the appropriate stream before the sequence begins. Batch sizing is managed to avoid deliverability impact and allows performance monitoring at each wave before scaling.

Suppressed profiles are assessed for recoverability based on original suppression reason. Hard bounces and spam complaints are excluded. Remaining profiles are prioritized by recency and engagement history before suppression.

Post-Survey Flow Integration

Stream A survey completers exit the win-back sequence immediately upon completion. Survey responses are used to route each profile into a personalized post-survey flow -- product recommendations tailored to their stated skin concerns, routine gaps, or reasons for lapsing.

This is the highest-value outcome of Stream A -- a customer who re-engages through the survey is expressing active interest, and the post-survey experience is designed to close that intent without requiring a discount offer.

Re-Permission Gate

At the end of Stream A, non-converting non-engagers reach a re-permission gate before any further communication. This protects deliverability and list quality while giving the audience one final opportunity to express continued interest or opt out cleanly. Profiles that do not engage with the re-permission ask are suppressed or removed from active sends.
