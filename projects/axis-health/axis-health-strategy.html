How the Flows Connect

  • Welcome → Browse Abandonment → Checkout Abandonment all exist in the pre-purchase funnel. They suppress on purchase.
  • Purchase triggers Post-Purchase. All pre-purchase flows immediately suppress when the first purchase is confirmed.
  • Post-Purchase flows through Day 14. The replenishment flow fires relative to the customer's supply end date. R1 sends 7 days before the supply end date, R2 sends on the supply end date. There is a clean gap between the last post-purchase email and the replenishment window with no overlap.
  • Replenishment → Winback handoff: If the 2-email replenishment flow completes and the customer still hasn't repurchased, they enter Winback after a 14-day gap. If they repurchase during replenishment, the cycle restarts from the next replenishment trigger.
  • No flow overlaps. A customer can only be in one active flow at a time. Purchase events always take precedence and exit/suppress all active flows.

Section 02
Industry Research Summary

Key findings across four research areas. These findings directly shape timing, tone, and message hierarchy for each email in both flows.

GLP-1 / Medical Weight Loss — Patient Journey

50%+
Drop off within 12 months
Real-world data (Denmark cohort, AJMC 2025) shows over 50% of semaglutide users discontinue within one year — with the steepest drop-off at months 3, 6, and 9. Month 1–2 is the most critical retention window.
Wk 2–4
When doubt peaks
Most patients notice appetite suppression within days, but measurable weight loss typically begins at weeks 4–8. The gap between "something feels different" and "I can see it working" is where doubt and dropout cluster.
3 Core
Dropout drivers
Side effects (nausea, fatigue in first 2–4 weeks), cost/ROI doubt ("this is expensive — is it working?"), and unrealistic early expectations. All three are email-addressable with the right messaging at the right moment.
Hope → ?
Emotional arc is non-linear
Patients typically move: Initial hope → First-week physical adjustment (side effects, nausea, fatigue) → Doubt if results feel slow → Relief when results arrive → Growing commitment after first noticeable milestone. Email must mirror this arc.

Sources: AJMC (2025), JAMA Network Open discontinuation cohort study, Lancet eClinicalMedicine (2026), Healthline GLP-1 overview, general clinical weight loss literature.

Strategy implication: The post-purchase flow must proactively address side effects and slow early results before the patient has a chance to feel like the medication "isn't working." Expectation-setting on Day 1 and a targeted check-in at Day 5–7 (when nausea often peaks) are non-negotiable.

Men's Sexual Health — Patient Journey

30 min
Time to first result (ED meds)
Sildenafil/tadalafil typically produce results within 30–60 minutes of dosing. This is very fast compared to GLP-1 — meaning the patient has early "proof" — but also creates a different problem: the "I don't need it every time" mindset that kills replenishment.
#1
Dropout trigger: embarrassment/stigma
Many men who purchase in the first place have delayed getting help for months or years. Post-purchase, the highest risk is a shame-spiral if the medication doesn't work perfectly the first time, or silent attrition when life gets busy.
Privacy
It's still top of mind post-purchase
Research on Hims/Ro patient behavior shows privacy concerns don't stop at checkout. Men worry about who sees their inbox, their statements, their delivery. Post-purchase email must reinforce discretion — subject lines, sender name, and packaging language all matter.
Soft
Replenishment mindset
Unlike subscription-minded customers, many ED medication users think in individual occasions ("I only use it when I need it"). Replenishment framing must meet them there — not push subscription, but help them see that running out creates anxiety and missed moments.

Sources: Hims/Ro patient journey content, telehealth ED retention analysis, general men's health behavioral research, PDE5 inhibitor clinical literature.

Strategy implication: For men, the post-purchase emotional goal is normalization, not celebration. The best email you can send a man who just bought ED medication is one that makes him feel like he made a smart, confident decision — not one that draws attention to it. Privacy + confidence is the lane, every time.

Telehealth Email Retention — Industry Benchmarks

How leading telehealth brands handle post-purchase email
  • Hims/Hers: Heavy on clinical reassurance, doctor-voice content, and subscription framing. Strength: authority. Weakness: can feel impersonal and cold.
  • Ro: Narrative-forward, warm tone, strong educational content post-purchase. Better emotional resonance. Lighter on urgency/replenishment conversion tactics.
  • Found/Calibrate (GLP-1): Community + accountability model. Post-purchase emails heavily focused on lifestyle integration, community check-ins, and coach touchpoints. High engagement but resource-intensive.
  • Keeps (men's health): Strong "you're not alone" normalization framing. Monthly reminders tied to supply, not dates — which tests better for replenishment.

Axis Health opportunity: Be warmer than Hims, more conversion-focused than Ro, and more private/discreet than all of them.

Replenishment Flow — What the Research Tells Us

  • Time-based triggers outperform behavior-based alone for medical products where consumption is predictable. A 30-day supply should be followed up at Day 25–28, not "when someone abandons a cart."
  • Subscription framing converts best for GLP-1 where it's clearly a long-term treatment — but needs to be positioned as "ongoing support" rather than a subscription box.
  • Single-refill framing converts better for men's health — subscription feels like commitment men aren't ready for. "Keep your supply ready" is a more comfortable ask.
  • Subject lines that reference supply depletion (e.g., "Your supply is running low") significantly outperform generic "reorder now" subject lines in telehealth categories.
  • 2-email replenishment sequences keep the approach clean and focused. R1 sends 7 days before the supply end date (upcoming reminder), R2 sends on the supply end date (the main conversion ask). Winback flow handles recovery for non-converters after the replenishment window closes.
Section 03
Post-Purchase Flow Strategy

What we're building, why, and how each lane differs in emotional arc and messaging approach.

Strategic Recommendation: 3 Emails Per Lane, 30-Day Arc

The post-purchase window for both lanes is 30 days. That's when the supply runs low and the replenishment trigger fires. Within that window, 3 emails is the right volume — enough to support the patient through the critical early stages without feeling like a bombardment from a brand they just trusted with a sensitive purchase.

The arc is: Welcome → Address doubt → Reinforce commitment → Prep for refill. Both lanes follow this arc, but the emotional color of each email is entirely different.

Timing Logic — Why These Days?

Email Send Timing Reasoning
E1 — Welcome/Confirmation Day 0 (immediate) Purchase anxiety is highest in the first hour. Confirm, reassure, set expectations before doubt can form. "Did I make the right decision?" — answer it before they ask.
E2 — Check-In / Side Effect Support Day 5 For GLP-1: nausea and early side effects typically peak at days 3–7 after first dose. This is the highest-risk dropout moment. For Men's Health: first use will likely have happened — this is the ideal moment to normalize the experience and reinforce confidence. Day 5 is specific enough to feel personal.
E3 — Progress Milestone Day 14 Two weeks in. Some early results may be visible (reduced appetite for women, confidence building for men). This is the right moment to celebrate the decision without overselling results. Also: day 14 is exactly when wavering patients are deciding whether to continue. This email needs to get there first.

How the Lanes Differ

Women's Weight Loss
GLP-1 / Medical Weight Loss
  • Emotional arc: Hope → adjustment (side effects) → early wins → renewed commitment
  • Primary anxiety: "Is this working? I don't feel different enough." Cost justification. Side effects making them second-guess.
  • Voice: Warm, supportive, like a doctor who's also a trusted friend. Non-judgmental about the yo-yo dieting past. Validating of the journey taken to get here.
  • What they need from email: Permission to be patient. Evidence that the process is working. Reassurance that side effects are temporary and normal.
  • Replenishment angle: "Your body is learning. Don't stop now." Continuation as investment in what's working.
  • Visually: Warm rose/blush accent. Gentle, soft. Not "pink washing" — more like quiet confidence.
Men's Health
ED / Sexual Performance
  • Emotional arc: Relief (they did it) → quiet confidence → reinforcement → habit formation
  • Primary anxiety: "Did this work the way it was supposed to?" Embarrassment if it didn't go perfectly. Privacy. "Do I really need to keep taking this?"
  • Voice: Calm, matter-of-fact, direct without being clinical. Zero judgment. Treats the customer like an adult who made a smart decision.
  • What they need from email: Low-key validation. Practical information. To not feel like they're part of a campaign about their insecurity.
  • Replenishment angle: "Always be ready. Don't run out when it matters." Running out creates stress — have it on hand before you need it.
  • Visually: Clean teal/slate. Minimal. Confident without being aggressive.

When and How to Introduce Replenishment CTA

The post-purchase flow is not a sales flow. It's a retention and onboarding flow. All three emails are patient-focused:

  • E1, E2, and E3 have zero sales CTAs. If a patient repurchases organically during this window (they liked it, they want more), the flow suppresses — but we're not selling during the first 30 days.
  • The dedicated Replenishment flow (triggered by supply end date) takes over for all conversion-focused messaging.
Section 04
Replenishment Flow Strategy

Separate flow or integrated? When to trigger? How to frame? This section makes the call.

Decision: Separate Replenishment Flow. Triggered by Supply End Date.

Why separate? The post-purchase and replenishment flows have different jobs and different emotional registers. Post-purchase is onboarding — building trust, managing expectations, normalizing the experience. Replenishment is conversion — getting the customer to act before their supply runs out. Running them as one long flow blurs both goals.

A standalone replenishment flow also gives us clean performance data. We need to know which emails drive reorders, not guess at it inside a larger flow.

Trigger: Date-based, tied to the customer's supply_end_date profile property passed from Axis Health's system to Klaviyo. R1 fires 7 days before the supply end date. R2 fires on the supply end date. This works across all supply lengths (monthly, 3-month, 6-month, 12-month) with no flow changes required.

Replenishment Flow Architecture

Email Timing (relative to supply end date) Mission
R1 — Upcoming Reminder 7 days before supply end date Your supply is almost out. Here's how to stay on track. Friendly, zero pressure. Warm and onboarding-adjacent.
R2 — Replenishment On supply end date Today's the day. Don't let the plan lapse. Clear CTA, direct framing. If they haven't reordered, this is the closer.

Messaging Angle — Women vs. Men

Women's Weight Loss — Replenishment
Continuity as Progress
  • Frame: Your body has been doing the work. Don't stop now. Stopping GLP-1 mid-program means weight often returns — this is clinically established and women need to know it early.
  • Tone: Empowering, not fear-based. "You started something real" — not "if you stop you'll undo everything."
  • Subscription push: Yes, lean into subscription or program framing. For GLP-1, ongoing treatment is medically appropriate and the customer likely expects it. Frame it as "staying in program" not "auto-renewal."
  • Objection to pre-empt: Cost. Acknowledge it, validate it, and remind them of what they're investing in — themselves, their health, the version of themselves they wanted when they started.
Men's Health — Replenishment
Always Be Ready
  • Frame: Don't let running out be the thing that kills the moment. Replenishment is practical, not committal. "Keep your supply topped up" — not "subscribe to your ED medication."
  • Tone: Practical. Calm. Brief. Men in this category respond to efficiency, not emotional appeals. Short emails win.
  • Subscription push: No hard subscription push. Offer single refill as primary CTA. Subscription as an optional upgrade if they want to simplify. Auto-ship framing can work ("set it and forget it") but shouldn't feel locked-in.
  • Objection to pre-empt: "I don't use it every time so I don't need to reorder." Counter: running out at the wrong moment creates stress. Better to have it and not need it.

Replenishment: If Customer Repurchases

  • Any repurchase event during the replenishment flow (Days 30–35) immediately suppresses all remaining replenishment emails.
  • The cycle resets: a new replenishment trigger is set based on the supply_end_date property from the new purchase.
  • After 3 consecutive repurchase cycles, consider whether this customer should be flagged as a VIP/loyalty segment for future flows (outside this scope, but flag for Michael).

Segment Entry Rules

Women's Weight Loss
Entry Criteria
  • Has purchased a weight loss / GLP-1 product
  • Identified as Women's lane (by product purchased or profile tag)
  • Has consented to marketing communications
  • Is not currently in an active conflicting flow
Men's Health
Entry Criteria
  • Has purchased a men's health / ED product
  • Identified as Men's lane (by product purchased or profile tag)
  • Has consented to marketing communications
  • Is not currently in an active conflicting flow

Global Suppression Rules

  • Purchase event (any): Immediately suppresses all active pre-purchase flows (Welcome, Browse Abandonment, Abandoned Checkout) and triggers Post-Purchase entry.
  • Repurchase during Post-Purchase: Suppresses remaining Post-Purchase emails. Replenishment timer resets from the new purchase date.
  • Repurchase during Replenishment: Immediately suppresses all remaining Replenishment emails. New replenishment trigger is set based on the supply_end_date property from the new purchase.
  • Unsubscribe: Exits all active flows globally. No further email contact until explicit re-consent.
  • No simultaneous flows: A customer should never be in Post-Purchase and Replenishment at the same time. The replenishment flow (triggered by supply_end_date) fires after the post-purchase sequence (Days 0/5/14) completes, ensuring a clean handoff with no overlap.

Post-Purchase → Replenishment Handoff

Scenario What happens
PP flow completes normally (Day 14), no repurchase Replenishment flow fires relative to supply_end_date. R1 sends 7 days before end date, R2 sends on end date. Clean gap between PP completion and replenishment window with no overlap.
Customer repurchases during PP flow (Day 5 or 14) Remaining PP emails suppressed. Replenishment timer resets based on supply_end_date from the new purchase.
Customer repurchases during Replenishment window All replenishment emails suppressed. New replenishment cycle begins, triggered by supply_end_date from the new purchase.
Replenishment completes with no repurchase (R2 sent) 14-day pause after R2. Winback flow activates.

Data Requirement — Action Item for Axis Health

Axis Health must pass a supply_end_date date property to each Klaviyo profile for the replenishment flow to trigger correctly. This is the date-based trigger that drives both R1 (7 days before supply end) and R2 (on supply end date). Without it, the replenishment flow cannot fire accurately across different supply lengths.

This property should be set at the time of purchase and updated with each renewal or new order. It must be passed as a Klaviyo profile property, not an event property, so it can be used as a time-based flow trigger. Flag this to Axis Health's technical team as a prerequisite before the replenishment flow goes live.

Lane Switching

If a customer purchases across both lanes (e.g., a household with products from both the Women's Weight Loss and Men's Health lines), each purchase triggers its own independent flow for that product category. They should not be combined or crossed. The Axis Health team should ensure lane identification is product-level, not account-level.

Primary Success Metric

The number that matters most: 90-day retention rate

For GLP-1, clinical research shows the biggest dropout risk is in the first 3 months. For men's health, 90 days is when habit formation is either established or not. The post-purchase and replenishment flows together should move the needle on: what % of first-time purchasers make a second purchase within 90 days.

Industry baseline for telehealth first → second purchase rate: 25–35% without lifecycle email. With a well-executed post-purchase + replenishment strategy, this should reach 40–55%. That's the benchmark Axis Health should hold this strategy to.

Recommended Testing Roadmap

  1. Launch with no tests. Let all emails run for 6–8 weeks to establish baseline performance before splitting.
  2. First test: Subject lines on PP-W2 and PP-M2 — the highest-stakes emotional emails. Test "practical" vs "empathetic" subject line approaches.
  3. Second test: R2 (replenishment email) framing — clinical angle ("stopping GLP-1 can affect your results") vs. emotional angle ("don't lose what you've gained") vs. practical angle ("it's time to refill").
  4. Third test: R2 with vs. without incentive — does adding a discount convert more customers, or does it erode margin with people who would have reordered anyway?
  5. Ongoing: Track and report per-flow at 30/60/90 days.
