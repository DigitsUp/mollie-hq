The Real Story

This isn't a farm brand.
It's a life brand.

Billy and Keira didn't move to Hawaii to start a business. They moved to raise their boys on real food, real land, and real values. The farm is the setting. The mission is the story.

And that story — choosing differently, betting on your family, building something that actually matters — resonates with millions of people who will never leave their zip code but deeply wish they could feel the way you do.

That's the audience. That's the opportunity. Not just people who want to buy organic fruit — people who want to be part of what you're building, even from the other side of the world.

"The most powerful thing you can do right now isn't grow your first crop. It's grow your audience while you wait for the harvest."

The strategy
Who's Watching

Five audiences.
All waiting for you.

You don't have to chase any of these people. When you tell your story authentically, they find you. Here's who they are and why they care.

01
The Inspired Parent
28–42 · Urban & Suburban
They're in the grind. Good job, good life on paper, but something feels off. They follow accounts that make them feel like a different life is possible — even if they never take the leap.
Why they care: You're living the version of their life they haven't been brave enough to try. You're not selling them anything. You're giving them permission to feel differently.
02
The Organic Family
Mom-led · 30–45 · Values-driven
They read labels. They buy organic. They think about what their kids eat. They've been looking for a farm brand they actually believe in — one with a real family behind it, not a corporation.
Why they care: You are what they've been looking for. When you have product, they'll be first in line. Until then, they'll evangelize your story.
03
The Surf & Outdoor Crowd
Surfers · Outdoor Enthusiasts · Lifestyle-led
North Shore Hawaii. Family. Waves. Living close to the land. This audience lives and breathes the culture you're building in. The farm-to-table ethos, the outdoor lifestyle, the family values — it all resonates deeply.
Why they care: You're living their dream with a family twist. Farm stay experiences, behind-the-scenes content, and brand partnerships land naturally here.
04
The Wellness Seeker
28–45 · Health-Conscious · Values-Led
They've been moving away from processed food for years. They grow herbs on their windowsill. They watch documentaries about food systems. They want to know where things come from and who grew them.
Why they care: You are the answer to the question they've been asking. A real family, real land, real food. When product exists, this audience becomes your most loyal customer.
05
The Young Couple Dreaming
22–32 · Aspirational · High Engagement
They're watching. They're saving. They're not sure what they want but they know it looks something like what you're building. High engagement, high share rate, the reason content goes viral.
Why they care: You're the dream they haven't named yet. They don't need to buy anything to become your most powerful marketing channel — they just need to feel it.
How You Build It

Six channels.
One connected story.

Each channel does a different job. Together they tell one story across the places people already spend their time — and none of it requires being a tech expert. Just showing up and being real.

📸
Instagram
The Window
Behind the scenes. The boys in the field. Keira in the kitchen. The land changing with the seasons. Short, beautiful, real. This is where people fall in love with what you're building before they even know your name.
Lever: Families, moms, outdoor enthusiasts, and people craving a slower life are already searching for this content. You don't need to find them — they'll find you.
🎥
YouTube
The Depth
The move. The first season. The failures and the wins. 10-minute episodes that feel like a show you can't stop watching. YouTube is the long game — it compounds over years and becomes your most valuable asset.
Lever: Ad revenue + brand deals once audience builds. Evergreen content that works while you sleep. The farm journal that never expires.
✉️
Letters from the Farm
The Newsletter
Weekly. Personal. Reads like a letter from a friend who moved to Hawaii and wants to bring you along. What's growing. What's failing. What the boys learned this week. What Keira cooked. Real life, beautifully told.
Lever: Sponsorship-ready at 10K subscribers. Product launch vehicle. The most loyal audience you'll build — people who chose to invite you into their inbox.
⚡
Email / Klaviyo
The Infrastructure
The engine under everything else. Pre-launch list building right now. Welcome series that tells the full story. Segmented sequences for product buyers, experience guests, and content fans — each getting something tailored.
Lever: The asset that survives every algorithm change. When product launches, the email list is what converts. Build it from day one.
🌿
Stay & Farm Experiences
The Premium Play
North Shore Hawaii. Surf in the morning. Farm in the afternoon. Keira's cooking at dinner. A week with the Busch family on the land they chose over everything else. This isn't a vacation — it's a transformation.
Lever: Agritourism is a massive and growing market. Build the waitlist now. Launch to the network first. Premium price, premium experience, word of mouth does the rest.
✍️
The Farm Journal
Blog & Long-Form Writing
Short stories from the farm. What you learned planting your first crop. What you fed the boys this week and why. How you chose Hawaii. Written simply, from the heart — no expertise required, just your real life on the page.
Why it matters: People search for this kind of life. "Farm to table family Hawaii." "Raising kids on a farm." "Organic fruit North Shore." Every post you write is a door that stays open forever — bringing new people to your story long after you wrote it.
🤝
Brand Partnerships
The Amplifier
Outdoor gear. Organic food. Family wellness. Surf brands. Baby and kids. Once the audience exists, the right brands will want to be part of your story. You choose who fits — and you don't need to chase anyone.
Lever: Meaningful partnerships only — brands that align with the values. Each one extends reach into new audiences without compromising the story.
