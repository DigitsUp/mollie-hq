Current Gaps

Where revenue is
being left behind.

A quick audit of handetizer.com reveals a brand with a strong visual identity and a genuinely differentiated product — but several structural gaps in the customer lifecycle that are costing repeat purchases and lowering lifetime value.

GAP 01
No Collection-Personalized Welcome
The email signup form has no preference capture. New subscribers receive a generic welcome — no branch for Dark vs. Light Collection preference, no scent-based onboarding that builds intent before the first purchase.
Opportunity
GAP 02
No Replenishment Trigger
Hand sanitizer is a consumable. A 1 oz bottle used daily runs out in roughly 4–6 weeks. There's no replenishment flow to catch buyers before they lapse — no timed prompt, no bundle upsell to the 6 or 12-pack.
Revenue Risk
GAP 03
Cart Abandonment Unaddressed
No active cart or browse abandonment recovery flow. With discovery packs priced at $36–$60, a well-timed 2–3 touch sequence with a low-friction incentive can meaningfully recover lost revenue on every campaign push.
Revenue Risk
GAP 04
Bundle Opportunity Untapped
The Bundle & Save structure is strong — but there's no automation nudging single-bottle buyers toward packs. Post-purchase should position the discovery six-packs as the obvious next step after trying a single scent.
Opportunity
GAP 05
No SMS Channel Active
Instagram and TikTok show clear community investment, but no SMS list capture exists. SMS is especially high-ROI for consumables brands — flash restocks, limited scents, and cart recovery see 3–5× higher open rates than email alone.
Opportunity
GAP 06
No Win-Back Sequence
Without a win-back flow, churned customers are invisible. For a brand with this level of product differentiation, a scent-nostalgia angle combined with a bundle incentive should be actively recovering lapsed buyers at 90–120 days.
Revenue Risk

00
Scent Quiz Popup + List Growth
3-step signup
⌄
The existing signup form captures email only — no preference data, no segmentation hook. We rebuild it as a 3-step Klaviyo popup: email capture → Dark or Light preference → SMS opt-in. The preference tag powers the entire welcome flow branch and informs every downstream communication. This is the foundation everything else runs on.
Step 1
Email capture — "Find your scent."
Exit-intent or 10s delay. Offer: 10% off first order. Copy leads with the Dark/Light collection contrast.
Step 2
Collection preference — Dark or Light?
Two-button choice. Tags subscriber in Klaviyo as dark_collection or light_collection. Powers welcome flow branching.
Step 3
SMS opt-in — "Get early access to new drops."
Optional step. SMS consent captured via Klaviyo's compliant double opt-in. Builds SMS list for future restocks and flash campaigns.

02
Browse Abandonment
2 emails
⌄
Fires when an identified subscriber views a product or collection page but doesn't add to cart. Low-friction, curiosity-led copy. No discount on Email 1 — we lead with scent storytelling. Incentive introduced on Email 2 only if no purchase.
+1 hr
That scent you were looking at…

03
Cart Abandonment
3 emails + 1 SMS
⌄
Fires on "Started Checkout" with no subsequent purchase. Three-touch sequence escalating from soft reminder to social proof to incentive. SMS fires alongside Email 3 for maximum recovery on high-value carts ($30+).
+1 hr
You left something behind.
Simple, clean recovery email. Cart contents pulled dynamically. No discount. Just a nudge with "Free shipping over $30" reminder.
+24 hr
Clean hands. Signature scent. Made for this.
Social proof email. Leads with the brand differentiator — scent-forward sanitizer vs. generic. Product benefit reinforcement. Mild urgency.
+48 hr
Still yours — plus a little something.
Final recovery email. Discount introduced for carts that haven't closed. Positions bundle upsell: "Add one more bottle and hit free shipping."
+48 hr
Your cart is waiting 🧴
SMS fires alongside Email 3. Short, punchy. Direct link back to cart. Sent only to SMS subscribers who haven't purchased.

04
Post-Purchase + Replenishment
5 emails · replenishment-timed
⌄
Fires after first purchase. Timed around the average consumable lifespan of a 1 oz spray bottle (~30 days daily use). Goals: delight on delivery, upsell to discovery pack, collect a review, and trigger replenishment before they run out. Branches at Email 3 based on what they bought — single bottle buyers get a pack upsell, pack buyers get a cross-collection nudge.
Day 1
Your scent is on its way.
Order confirmation-adjacent. Brand storytelling, shipping info, what to expect when it arrives. Sets up product experience.
Day 7
How's your new scent treating you?
Post-delivery check-in. Asks how they're liking it. Introduces the other collection subtly — "if you went Dark, here's what Light feels like."
Day 14
[Single buyers] Try the full collection. [Pack buyers] What else is in your collection?
Branched upsell. Single bottle → discovery six-pack positioned as the smart next step. Pack buyers → cross-collection or a new scent from the opposite collection.
Day 21
Leave us a review — take 60 seconds.
Review request. UGC-friendly framing. Links to product page. Optional photo/video review prompt for social proof pipeline.
Day 30
Running low? You know what to do.
Replenishment trigger. Timed to ~30 days after delivery. Positions bundle as the smarter refill — more scents, better value, free shipping. Urgency-light CTA.
05
Win-Back
3 emails + 1 SMS
⌄
Fires at Day 90 post-purchase with no repeat order. Scent-nostalgia angle — reminds them what they loved. Escalates to a bundle incentive at Day 105. Final SMS at Day 120 before profile is moved to suppression to protect deliverability.
Day 90
Still smelling good out there?
Light-touch nostalgia email. References their collection preference. No discount — just a reminder the scent is still there waiting.
Day 105
We miss you. Here's 15% to come back.
Win-back incentive introduced. Positions the discovery six-pack as the best value comeback. Bundle messaging reinforced.
Day 120
Final call — offer expires tonight.
Last chance email + SMS. Hard expiry on the offer. If no purchase, profile moves to unengaged suppression to protect sender health.

Segmentation

Smart lists.
Deliverability built in.

We build and maintain the core segment library from day one. These power both the automation triggers and any broadcast campaigns sent during an active retainer.

Dark Collection
Tagged dark_collection at popup. All subscribers who expressed preference for bold/warm scents.
Light Collection
Tagged light_collection at popup. All subscribers who expressed preference for bright/playful scents.
Single Bottle Buyers
Placed Order with order value under $20. Primary upsell target for discovery six-pack.
Pack Buyers
Placed Order with 6 or 12-pack SKU. Cross-collection and replenishment targets.
Engaged (90 days)
Opened or clicked at least 1 email in the last 90 days. Primary broadcast send list.
Unengaged (90+ days)
No open/click in 90+ days. Suppressed from broadcasts, fed into win-back flow.
SMS Subscribers
Double opt-in SMS consent captured at popup step 3. Used for cart recovery and new drop alerts.
Replenishment Window
Purchased 25–35 days ago with no repeat order. Used to amplify replenishment flow with broadcast backup.
