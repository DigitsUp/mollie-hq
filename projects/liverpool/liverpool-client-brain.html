The Story
What's Working
  • Total attributed revenue up +73% YoY — $79K vs $45.7K same week last year. The flow engine is doing the heavy lifting and the week-over-week recovery (+42.5%) shows momentum building into Q2.
  • Flows are the revenue spine — $48.6K of $79K total is automated. Welcome Series 2026 alone drove $9.1K this week at a 6.2% CVR. That's a best-in-class welcome sequence.
  • 52.1% open rate — well above industry average for apparel. Engaged list, relevant sends. The Spring send cadence (Superbloom, Getaway Looks, Best Tops) is landing.
What Needs Attention
  • Email campaign conversions down 43.6% YoY (132 vs 234). Recipients are down 22.9% — smaller list reach is the likely primary driver. Worth reviewing send-list qualification criteria.
  • Email campaign revenue down 33.6% YoY ($23.3K vs $45.7K LY). The send volume gap accounts for some of this — but the 30-day campaign revenue trend (−84.9% YoY over 30 days) is the bigger concern to address in Q2 strategy.
  • 30-day attributed revenue −34% YoY ($235.6K vs $356.8K LY). Flows are actually up +24.8% over 30 days — but campaigns are carrying a -84.9% YoY deficit that's dragging the total.
Recommended Actions
  • Lead the call with the +73% YoY attributed revenue win — that's the headline, and it's real.
  • Audit the campaign list qualification approach — fewer recipients but same send frequency could signal over-suppression or aggressive smart-sending thresholds.
  • Win-back 2026 is in build — prioritize launch. The winback layer is the fastest path to closing the 30-day YoY gap without increasing send volume.
