The Story
What's Working
  • Total attributed revenue up +64.4% YoY — $166.1K vs $101.0K same week last year. Biggest number we've seen from this account. Both campaigns and flows are up YoY — a clean sweep.
  • Campaign revenue nearly doubled YoY — $79.7K vs $41.8K LY (+90.6%). Email list reach grew from 600K to 1.44M recipients — the list build is paying off. Send volume + quality = win.
  • Flows up +45.9% YoY — $86.4K this week vs $59.2K same week LY. TTCMYU (Toki loyalty) and Welcome Series are the two biggest movers. Retention engine is firing.
  • 55.0% email open rate — above industry average for apparel and up from last week. 16 email campaigns deployed this week with strong engagement across the board.
What Needs Attention
  • Campaign RPR is diluted at scale — top email campaign (01KN31F, $21.2K) sent to 250K recipients with only 99 conversions (0.04% CVR). High volume, low conversion rate. Suggests broad untargeted sending vs. surgical sends LY. May be worth A/B testing a high-intent segment.
  • SMS revenue is still modest — $10.8K on 178K recipients. Click-through is strong (20K+ clicks) but conversions are only 59. SMS is driving traffic but not closing. Consider a dedicated SMS promo or offer to lift conversion.
  • Flows WoW +6.0% — healthy, but the TTCMYU loyalty flow is carrying a disproportionate share ($14.9K). If that flow has a promotional push baked in, the underlying baseline may be softer. Worth isolating TTCMYU to understand organic vs triggered cadence.
Recommended Actions
  • Lead the call with the +64.4% YoY total attributed revenue — $166.1K is the strongest week on record for this account. That's the headline.
  • Investigate TTCMYU loyalty flow performance ($14.9K this week) — confirm if this is evergreen or event-triggered. If evergreen, it's a model for expanding loyalty touchpoints.
  • Review campaign RPR — 16 emails sent with $69K revenue is good, but the top sender (250K recips, $21.2K) converted at just 0.04%. A segmented send to high-intent shoppers on the same creative may lift CVR meaningfully.
  • Win-back 2026 is in build — prioritize launch. With flows already at $86.4K/week, adding a healthy winback layer could push weekly attribution north of $200K.

Campaign Commentary
What's Working
  • Campaign revenue nearly doubled YoY. $79.7K vs $41.8K same week 2025 — a +90.6% improvement. More sends, more recipients (1.44M vs 600K), and the cadence is working. Q2 is off to a strong start.
  • Top email (01KN31F) drove $21.2K alone. 250K recipients, 49.6% open rate, 99 conversions. Best single-email output this account has seen. Whatever creative and segment was behind this deserves a repeat.
  • Open rates are exceptional. Three emails above 49% open rate in the same week. The send cadence is well-calibrated — no fatigue signal visible yet.
What's Not
  • Email CVR is still thin at scale. Top email (01KN31F): 99 conversions on 250K sends = 0.04% CVR. LY top email got 85 convs on 240K sends (0.04%). Volume is up but per-send conversion rate is flat. Suggest testing a high-intent segment version of the top-performing send.
  • SMS RPR lags email. Best SMS ($0.109 RPR on 01KN5W) is good for SMS, but the email top performer ($21.2K / 250K = $0.085 RPR) is not far behind despite being broadcast. SMS list quality may be the constraint.
Action Item
  • Identify and preserve the creative/segment behind 01KN31F — $21.2K from a single email is a playbook moment. Replicate the approach for April sends.
  • Consider a dedicated high-intent segment send on top email creative. If 5% of 250K were in an "engaged buyer" segment and converted at 0.5%, that's $5–8K in incremental revenue from one resend.

Flow Commentary
What's Driving Revenue
  • TTCMYU (Toki Loyalty SMS) is the surprise #1 flow — $14.9K this week on 1,666 recipients at a 6.48% CVR. This is a loyalty-triggered SMS flow generating outsized revenue. It was also #1 last week at $17.0K. This is a best-in-class loyalty mechanic.
  • Welcome Series 2026 (UaeNDJ) drove $14.1K this week — 91 conversions on 986 recipients = 9.23% CVR. Up from last week's $11.5K. The welcome sequence is at peak performance — don't touch it.
  • Flow revenue +45.9% YoY — $86.4K this week vs $59.2K same week LY. The automation engine is compounding. More flows, better sequences, deeper coverage of the customer journey.
  • Added2Cart (Sku3EU) generated $12.1K — 64 conversions on 4,327 recipients (1.48% CVR). Healthy signal that the cart recovery sequence is well-optimized across both email and SMS.
What Needs Attention
  • TTCMYU concentration risk — $14.9K of $86.4K total flow revenue (17%) is coming from one loyalty SMS flow. If this is triggered by a promotional mechanic, it may not recur reliably. Worth confirming the trigger logic.
  • Win-back 2026 still in build — XwcDKX (Win-back 2026) is processing but $2.7K on 3,376 recipients suggests it's live but not yet optimized. The messaging or timing may need a review pass.
  • Browse Abandonment (U9NPLQ) at 0.35% CVR — 8,520 recipients, $4.2K. The volume is there; the conversion rate is not. This flow likely needs a creative refresh or urgency mechanic (low stock, limited time).
