Campaign Insights & Recommendations
New vs Return Split
All campaigns must segment New vs Return. Sending the same message to both audiences wastes budget, erodes brand trust with buyers, and inflates unsubscribes from prospects getting irrelevant content.
Engagement Throttling
Throttle campaign sends by engagement tier. Never broadcast to the full list. Cold and inactive segments hurt deliverability for the entire sending domain.
Inactive Re-engagement
Test plain-text sends to inactive segments every 2 weeks. Plain text consistently outperforms designed emails for lapsed audiences — feels personal, bypasses promotional folder filtering.
Loyalist Cadence
22,506 loyalists (4+ orders) can receive 2–3 campaign sends per week. They are opted-in, engaged, and responsive. Do not under-send to your best customers.

New Tool Recommendation
Klaviyo CDP — Product Recommendations

Klaviyo's CDP feature identifies the most common co-purchased and next-purchase products from real order data. Example from a client account: 18% of customers who purchased a specific item also purchased a second item in the same order. On the next order, 5% returned and purchased the original item in a different color. This behavioral intelligence powers cross-sell flows with genuine signal rather than manual product curation. Recommend evaluating once the core flow architecture is stable — Month 4 or 5.

Flow Hand-Off Logic

The architecture only works if flows communicate correctly. These suppression rules prevent conflicting messages and ensure every customer is in exactly one flow at a time.

Welcome →
Suppressed by first purchase. Customer exits immediately into post-purchase flow.
Browse Abandon →
Suppressed by cart add or purchase. Immediately exits if either event fires.
Cart Abandon →
Suppressed by checkout initiation or purchase. Cart → checkout is a natural progression, not abandonment.
Checkout Abandon →
Suppressed by purchase only. Stays active until order placed or flow completes.
Post-Purchase →
Suppresses all abandonment flows on order confirmation. Restarts on next purchase.
Winback →
Suppressed by any purchase or open + click. Even a single re-engagement exits the flow immediately.
Dynamic profile updates

Segment tags and category properties should update dynamically throughout the customer lifecycle — not set once at signup. A sport buyer who later purchases a boardshort should be reclassified. Every flow should always read the most current profile state, not a tag set six months ago. This is the single most common implementation failure in Klaviyo programs of this complexity.

On Sequencing

The order matters. Welcome and abandonment flows are built before post-purchase because they generate the data — purchase history, category preferences, loyalty status — that makes post-purchase personalization possible. You can't segment a post-purchase flow on purchase category if you don't have purchase data yet. Foundation first, depth second.
